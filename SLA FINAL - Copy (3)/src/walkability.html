Walkability — Key Concepts

Walkability measures how friendly an area is for walking: safety, comfort, convenience and access to destinations.

Measurements

  • Connectivity — block lengths & intersection density
  • Diversity — mix of uses within walking distance
  • Density — people & activities per area
  • Safety — vehicle speeds, visibility & lighting

Tools & Metrics

Walk Score, pedestrian audits and simple counts can quantify walkability for class exercises.

Tip: measure one street's crossings, sidewalk width and nearby amenities for a short demo.

Walk Audit — Simple Checklist

  1. Are sidewalks continuous and wide enough?
  2. Are crossings frequent and well marked?
  3. Is there shade and seating?
  4. Are vehicle speeds calm (≤30 km/h)?
  5. Are destinations within 10–15 minute walk?

Short Case Study

Low-cost changes — curb extensions, extra trees, clearer crosswalks and pop-up parks — often increase foot traffic and local sales.
