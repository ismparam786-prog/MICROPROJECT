Walkability — Key Concepts

Walkability measures how friendly an area is for walking: safety, comfort, convenience and access to destinations.

Measurements

Connectivity
block lengths & intersection density
Short blocks and multiple route options reduce walking distance and improve route choice.
72%
Safety
vehicle speeds, visibility & lighting
Calm vehicle speeds, good sightlines and lighting make walking feel safe day and night.
56%
Diversity
mix of uses within walking distance
A mix of housing, shops, offices and services within a short walk increases everyday walking.
64%
Green & Shade
trees, shade & comfort
Trees and seating improve thermal comfort and encourage walking outdoors.
48%

Quick Tips — Small changes, big results

▸

Shorten crossing distances with curb extensions, add trees and seating, reduce car speeds and mark crossings — low-cost changes that make big differences.

Quick Quiz

Walk Audit — Interactive Checklist

  1. Are sidewalks continuous, wide, and barrier-free?
  2. Are crossings frequent, safe, and well-marked?
  3. Is there shade and seating?
  4. Is there seating and resting spots?
  5. Are vehicle speeds calm (≤30 km/h)?
  6. Are daily destinations within a comfortable 10–15 minute walk?
  7. Is the area well-lit for evening or early-morning walking?
  8. Are pedestrian signs, signals, and wayfinding easy to understand?
  9. Is the walking environment clean and well-maintained?
  10. Are there trees, greenery, or pleasant surroundings?
  11. Is the walking experience comfortable, safe, and enjoyable overall?
